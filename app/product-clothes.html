What is a handpan? A handpan is a handcrafted musical instrument invented in the 21st century. It looks like an inside-out steel drum, a turtle shell, a wok or a UFO. Part art and part instrument, the handpan is often referred to as a "sound sculpture". Each handpan is completely unique, bearing the hammer marks of its artisanal making process. Peaceful and relaxing, the handpan is particularly well-suited for music therapy and meditation.

How does a handpan work? A handpan consists of two bowls of steel glued together and tuned into a diatonic musical scale. The top shell of the handpan has a center note (referred to as "ding" by the inventors) which can be either dented outward (it would be called "apex") or inward ("inpex"). The "tone fields" are the notes of the scale (we also call them "dimples"). They are located all around the center note and played up and down in a zig-zag pattern. The bottom shell of the handpan has a hole (called "Gu"). This opening activates a deep bass note.
